Grove Lab Research

Bridging AI and Experimental Virology

At Grove Lab, we harness cutting-edge artificial intelligence alongside classical experimental virology to investigate the molecular mechanisms of viruses. Our focus is viral entry glycoproteins, but our combined computational and experimental approach can be extended to any viral proteins.

AI-Enabled Discovery

Artificial intelligence is revolutionizing molecular virology, enabling us to predict viral protein structures, infer evolutionary relationships, and identify novel targets for vaccines or therapies. Our AI-driven research includes:

  • Viro3D: A comprehensive database of virus protein structure predictions, expanding the structural coverage of viral proteins by 30 times using AlphaFold and ESMFold.
  • Structural Evolution: Mapping glycoprotein structures across the Flaviviridae to reveal deep evolutionary relationships and novel fusion mechanisms.
  • Functional Annotation: Using predicted structures to infer protein function, helping to characterize viral proteins with previously unknown roles.

Good-old-fashioned Experimental Virology

Our experimental virology research grounds computational insights in real-world biology. We apply a range of classical virological techniques to explore viral entry, host interactions, and evolution. Our investigations focus on:

  • Viral Entry Mechanisms: Understanding how viral glycoproteins interact with host receptors to drive infection, including how mutations fine-tune entry efficiency and immune evasion.
  • Host-Virus Interactions: Investigating how host factors like cholesterol-sensing receptors regulate viral infectivity and determine cellular tropism.
  • Evolution and Adaptation: Exploring how viral proteins evolve under immune pressure and structural constraints, shaping entry pathways and resistance mechanisms.
